The idea in plain language

Most brands are not short of dashboards. They are short of dependable interpretation. If Shopify, GA4, and paid media platforms tell different stories, planning becomes guesswork. Arcmedian exists to close that gap.

Operating model

A four-layer system designed for commercial use, not analytics theatre.

The practice is organised around a clear practical model: audit the measurement layer, reconcile conflicting signals, read for meaningful pattern, and turn the result into a weekly memo that a founder or small marketing team can actually use.

01

Measurement integrity

Connect commerce, analytics, and channel data; check event firing, data freshness, UTM hygiene, and structural breaks before performance is interpreted.

02

Reconciliation and confidence

Compare revenue, conversion, and traffic signals across systems, assign a confidence level to the KPI itself, and isolate likely causes where disagreement appears.

03

Pattern intelligence

Read the data for shift, drift, anomaly, and trend rather than merely reporting what happened. The purpose is to detect decision-relevant movement, not produce visual noise.

04

Weekly action memorandum

Translate the signal into a ranked, concise brief: what can be trusted, what likely changed, and what deserves attention next week.

Why it is different

The work starts where most analytics services stop.

Many firms report on platform outputs as they are given. Arcmedian works to a stricter standard: before advising on performance, it asks whether the measurement layer itself is internally coherent.

That creates a more unusual offer for smaller commerce brands: bespoke measurement review, cross-system reconciliation, and decision support in one compact practice.
